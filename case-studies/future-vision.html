Background

What is it?

Future Vision was a research and design project completed by myself and two other HCI students at Drexel University. The steps we took illustrate some of the methods human-centered design professionals may take when collecting qualitative information and designing a product.

Through practicing empathy, ideation and testing, we were able to obtain valuable qualitative insights into the way people travel and the variables that negatively affect their travel plans.

My Role

I assisted the team in conducting qualitative interviews, participated in group brainstorming and brain-netting session, and was the UI designer.

Empathy

1) Our Assumption

The group discussed our experiences with travel, as a broad term, as in going from "Point A" to "Point B". As a group, we surmised that traveling and navigating was, for the most part, unburdened by intense feelings.

2) Other Opinions

Over the course of three days, group members individually conducted qualitative interviews with participants via Zoom. Interviews were approx 30 minutes in length, with participants ranging in backgrounds, career statuses and ages.

3) Feedback

We found, no matter how brief the trip may be, traveling isn't easy for everybody. Those working multiple jobs or those with toddlers and/or children are two examples of where traveling can be stressful.

4) Problem Statement

Addressing the stressors that occur during and planning for a trip is where we redirected our attention. The problem statement decided was, "People are overwhelmed by the variables that can affect their travels".

Ideation

Brainstorming

Brain-netting was used to develop ideas based on the feedback from the qualitative interviews. The group produced 47 ideas and brainstorming was used to expand, clarify and group ideas togethor.

(SB-1) tracked and collected ideas from group members.
(SB-2) consolidated ideas by likeness and themes.

Initial Consolidation

We narrowed down the pool of ideas to 25 by combining similar ideas and and themes.

Group Vote

Final 3 Ideas

We voted on the remaining 25 ideas and decided on "Travel Wallet", "Realtime-GPS", and "Future Vision". These designs ideas would move onto the prototyping stage where the group began designing low-to-mid fidelity wireframes. I was selected to create the prototype for Future Vision.

Prototyping Tools

Future Vision tackled traffic and weather related challenges drivers may face in two locations simultaneously. Due to the sheer amount of information, I scaled down and designed my prototype based on weather forecasts for departure and arrival locations. I primarily used Sketch, Invision and Craft (plugin) to design the prototype.

Feedback Details

Current Improvements

  • • Add location of each event listing on the Home screen
  • • Provide clarity in presentation of arrival vs. departure location for weather details
  • • Improve distinction between the Itinerary button vs Edit Travel button
  • • Replace icon for screenshot feature, or improve feature to help users better understand what is happening
  • • Increase duration of screenshot features popup for prototype use only
  • • Add Fahrenheit distinction on Weather Details screen

Possible Future Fails

  • • The purpose of the Weather Details handlebar went unnoticed and/or was not understood
  • • Consider creating more than one prototype for various devices; prototype was specific to an Android user leaving some functions confusing to iPhone users
