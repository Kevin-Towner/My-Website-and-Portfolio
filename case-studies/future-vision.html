Background

Future Vision is a project that was completed in December, 2020, by myself and two other students at Drexel University. Future Vision was our capstone project for INFO-508, Information Innovation Through Design Thinking. The group originally decided to design a navigation-based application. Upon the completion of early steps in the design process, the group decided to focus on the stress and anxiety-inducing factors that can happen during a trip.

Process

Photo by Matt Hardy - https://www.pexels.com/@matthardy

Empathy

Prior to conducting any research, the group reflected on personal experiences relating to planning or going through the process of taking a trip. As a whole, the group surmised that traveleing is, for the most part, unburdened by intense feelings. From an empathetic approach, the group decided to talk to 8 people, in loosely-scripted interactions, to yield transparent and genuine information on the way others view navigation. The discussions were held with participants of varrying careers, experiences, geographic locations, ages and ethnicities. The group learned that the act of planning for and navigating between multiple destinations caused some levels of stress and anxiety for most participants regardless of how extensive the trip may be.

Ideation

• Brain-netting

To further explore the problem statement, the group utilized brain-netting and group brainstorming as a primary means of expanding upon the ideas gained from the discussions held during the empathy stage. By brain-netting, group members idividually gathered 17 ideas, 51 ideas total, that could assist with the problem statement. Each group was assigned a color post-it note color and submitted their ideas, via a post-it, to the online Stormboard. In the end, the group wound up with 47 solution ideas. (10.17.21 - 10.19.21)

• Collaborative Review

Group members met online via Zoom and collaboratively reviewed the solution ideas gathered in the stormboard. The review consisted of questions, answers and free-flow discussion with vritually no boundaries in regard to ideas or critiques. The exchange of ideas allowed us to weed out inadvertent duplicates and update the ideas' titles.
