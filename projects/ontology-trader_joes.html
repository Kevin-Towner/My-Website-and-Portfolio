Background

Clueless About Beer

Is that guy serious?

I was talking to an older gentleman and feel I was led into a pseudo-intellectual trap. The man asked what I was drinking and upon my reply we shared a couple sentences about its popularity. After asking me if I enjoy Blue Moon (I do enjoy Blue Moon), the man replied, "Huh..I don't get what's so special about those Belgian beers...", and walked way with a bit of a snobbish vibe.

Can't defend something I know nothing about.

I realized, even if I was inclined to put forth the effort to defend my love for drinking Blue Moon, I barely know anything about the study of beer or beer craftsmanship. Looking back at that time, I decided to create an ontology describing the relationship between individual beers, and their respective styles.

Learning Protege

Since I'm still learning about ontologies, OWL and Protege, I figured Trader Joe's unique beer selection would be a good place to build and become acquainted with ontologies and the Protege software.

Classes

Name and Style

Classes explain content

Beer Styles and Beer Names are the classes that will group our ground-level elements togethor. Although I contemplated adding individual beer styles as subclasses, I created a class named "Beer Style" which I may change in the future.

Individuals

Ground-level Instances

(IN-1) Names of beers at Trader Joe's

Individuals as instances

Individuals are instances of ground-level elements. In the image (IN-1) beer names are individuals under class Beer_Name.

Properties

Data Properties & Object Properties

Data Properties

Data properties, "ABV" and "Brewery", were added to individuals classified as Beer Names. In the image (DP-1) the data property assertions tell us that Black Toad (Beer Name) has an ABV of 5.9 and the brewery is Back Toad Brewing Company in California.

(DP-1), Beer Name's data properties

Object Property / "is_a"

Object Properties connect instances togethor. In the image (OP-1), object-property, "is_a" is describing the relationship between Black Toad (Beer Name) and English Brown Ale (Beer Style) - Black Toad "is a(n)" English Brown Ale.

(OP-1), Beer Style is a Beer Style.

Wrap Up

Conclusion

What did I walk away with?

While I may not have learned a lot about Blue Moon, or its ingrediants, nor have I walked away with Blue Moon defending abilities, I have learned a lot of information about other beers offered at Trader Joe's. I've also learned how to design domain ontologies in Protege. Going forward, I may continue adding to this ontology with additional properties and possibly restructuring the beer styles into subclasses of "TJ Beers"
