Usability and Heuristic Evaluation

Alliance for Historic Wyoming is an organization that aims to ensure the preservation of historic and monumental locations relevant to the state of Wyoming. As a young organization, they needed help with redesigning their website. My role was to provide usability and heuristic information to help guide the website redesign.

Kevin Towner•2 minute read

Introduction

Historic Wyoming Logo

Initial contact

On January 3rd, 2022, I had a brief interview with a key stakeholder within the organization to discuss details of the position, as well as learn more about the organization's goals. I was informed that the website was built with Squarespace, and some of the website content was outdated. Specific to my role, the stakeholder informed me that they were looking for guidance on making the website user friendly. I was also informed that space for more promotional content was important to the organization.

Problem statement

  • • Historic Wyoming's key stakeholders wish for their website to be more user friendly, because visitors are not fully aware of the organization's value, due to some minor usability flaws.

Usability Categories

Usability analysis

I developed a usability spreadsheet on Historic Wyoming's website with various criteria and standards that exemplify good usability practices. I also included a relevant organization, Preserve Montana, and an indirectly relevant organization, National Trust for Historic Preservation, in the spreadsheet.

Usability Scores

usability spreadsheet with various criteria graded on a 1-10 scale

Scoring usability criteria

After completing the usability analysis on the three websites, I created a separate spreadsheet with matching criteria as a score sheet. Scores coincide with feedback from the usability analysis so Historic Wyoming's stakeholders may quickly pinpoint the strengths and weaknesses of their website, as well as competing websites.
