Prototyping Assignment

The group and I were tasked with wireframing a web app of a zoo and designing a high fidelity prototype. With limited research and overall contributors, we put togethor a prototype, inspired by hypothetical competitors, demonstrating the ticket purchasing process with other tertiary functionalities included.

Specifications:

  • • Ticket Buying Experience
  • • Viewable activites and zoo events

Wireframes

Wireframing

We sketched high level wireframes with ideas taken from other websites (Treetop Adventure, Central Park Zoo, and New York Aquarium). We decided to include a navigation bar and footer with similar colors and layouts to the sites just referenced.

We also sketched rudimentary input forms and checkout screens for the ticket purchasing parts of the prototype. We took note that many of the comparable websites used similar layout and structure to each other.

Feedback

Feedback Summary

Most of the feedback revolves around a lack of information on specific screens, especially on the ticketing and purchasing sections of the prototype.

Feedback

  • • Maybe switch the layout of the homepage so that the top tabs provide easy access for zoo visitors with tabs such as tickets, volunteering, learn more etc.
  • • Include ages for ticket classifications (children under 12, seniors 65+, etc) on tickets page
  • • Do the times grey out or get removed from the page if that time slot is sold out?
  • • Petting zoo was in the cart but I didn't see when/where/how that was added
  • • Show which credit cards are accepted on the payment page (Visa, MasterCard, AmEx, etc)
  • • Provide information when purchasing tickets about how the tickets will be presented - do they need to be printed or is there a digital copy that can be used at the zoo and is an ID needed to confirm tickets
