Future Vision

The act of going from Point A to Point B, especially in our modern times, can be met with anxiety and stress. Within the timeframe allowed, the group and I were able to use empathy, ideation, experimentation, and testing methods to produce a weather-based prototype called Future Vision - Weather & Travel.

Introduction

Design thinking methods

Using research methods popular in human centered design, the group and I designed a prototype which aims to assist users in their travels. Through empathising with travelers, identifying a problem statement, ideating through solutions, group experimentation, and testing design solutions with research participants, we came up with Future Vision.

Medium fidelity screen from Future Vision design

Empathy

Forming Assumptions

Through intergroup communication, group members and I discussed and shared our personal opinions on travel. Feelings towards the topic in relation to helpful applications, and actual experiences were shared verbally as well as recorded on individual activity logs. Our group considered personal experiences and initially surmised that these processes are simple, easy and unburdened by intense feelings.

Interviews

Each group member conducted 2 to 3 information interviews/conversations with friends and/or family on the topic of travel to guage our initial assumption on travel. Steps people take during travel, behaviors and motivations on the act of traveling, and tools travelers may use to aid in arriving at their destinations were discussed.

Insights and surprises.

  • • Planning for and navigating between destinations causes anxiety and stress
  • • Mobile planning and navigation tools are considered reliable and trusted
  • • Enhanced personalization options will create added value to planning and navigation tools

Problem Statement

Nearly all participants indicated that they worry about the possibility of things going wrong during their trips. We also learned that mobile navigation applications are a trusted source of help despite the desire for additional functionalities to aid in participants' trips. People are overwhelmed by the variables that can affect their travels.

Group Ideation

Brainstorming with Stormboard

Over the course of 1 week, each group member contributed a number of ideas to the brain-netting (online brainstorming) session. The group then worked to narrow down the ideas to 25 through two rounds of group voting.

Final 25

Once the 25 ideas were decided on, we worked to label and properly name each of the ideas in preparation for organizing them into a spreadsheet and listing perceived promising as well as negative aspects about each idea.

Experiment & Test

Peer evaluation

Each group member created a prototype for their respective ideas, and conducted a peer reviewed walkthrough. Through evaluating each other's prototype we offered feedback, and created prototype test plans.

Obtaining feedback

Test sessions were facilitated via Zoom. Each included with an introduction, overview of the testing goals, explanation of interviewer and observer roles, and request for consent to record. Interviewer roles were assumed by the team member that designed each prototype.
