Learning Pixel Art Through Sci-Fi Influences

Creating pixel art, animal 'synthetics', each with sci-fi inspired information describing its purpose in a futuristic society. Ideas and inspiration are partially from the works of popular science fiction literature and media.

Kevin Towner•ongoing project•2 minute read
Replicant Zhora, from the movie Blade Runner (1982), handling a synthetic snake.

Introduction

Pixel art

When drawing a blank on creative ideas, I always feel tackling side challenges is a great way to gain momentum for creative inspiration. I've found pixel art to be, not only an activity that sparks creativity, but something fun and engaging to learn. While one may not have as many tools as somebody engaged in another form of digital art, pixel art teaches you how to do more with less.

Why the science fiction angle?

The science fiction and future cyberpunk angle allows for maximum creativity, bordering on incomprehensible levels of creativity. Similar to brainstorming, ideas revolving around the pixel art focus on quantity over quality and are not held to high levels of scrutiny.

Cyberpunk and science fiction are awesome because they tend to incorporate retro aesthetics, fantastical storylines, and self-actualizing subject material. Let's just roll with it!

Electric Frogs

Feasting on spotted lantern flies by a body of water

Artificial animals as a form of habitat control

Synthetic frogs are commonly used to deal with the spread of insects in rural areas. Agile and inexpensive, synthetic frogs are easy to identify by the way their organs flash with bright colors before consuming their prey.

While many city residents prefer the use of synthetic cats or other mammalian predators to deal with the growing insect population, synthetic frogs have proven to be more useful in areas that foster naturalistic biodegration.

Electric Cats

"I own this space and damn you for thinking otherwise. Now, give me a headscratch and don't make me say it twice"

Synthetic cats

Synthetic cats are used primarily for companionship and extermination. Designed to kill certain rodents and insects, they are an artifical animal of choice for people living in heavily populated areas. With an average 'life' span of 10 years, owning a synthetic cat is quite expensive despite being artifical.

Artifical cats who have been conditioned to killing invasive species will sport a green stripe on their tails while cats with a pink stripe have been conditioned to live the life of somebody's companion. These versatile artificals are a status symbol of high-class society. Many of the world elite donate 'X' amount of money per year specifically for articial cats to help clean up low-income sectors of the sprawl.
